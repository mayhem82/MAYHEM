FACTUALISM DOCTRINE

Only verifiable facts propagate. Everything else is noise. This is not a preference. It is a survival requirement.

EXCLUDES BY DESIGN:

  • Opinions, beliefs, explanations
  • Intent claims, motivations, character assessments
  • Probability estimates, reasonable assumptions
  • Common sense interpretations

Not because they are false—but because they cannot be enforced.

CRITICAL RULES

NO INFERENCE RULE: Even correct inference is excluded because it cannot be enforced. Inference is where institutions escape accountability.

CONTEXT IS NOT DEFENCE: Context does not erase facts. Explanations do not weaken facts. Context may be logged but never overrides evidence.

TIME IS FACT: Delay, silence, non-response, process duration are primary evidence, not background detail.

LANGUAGE IS THREAT: Euphemisms, softening language, passive voice, policy phrasing, "lessons learned" rejected. Facts recorded as events, not interpretations.

FACT VS CLAIM THE HARD LINE

A CLAIM is something asserted. A FACT is something that survives verification. A claim becomes a fact ONLY when it achieves PF(H,E)=1. Until then, it does not exist inside MAYHEM. Binary is intentional: gradients invite negotiation, probabilities invite debate, scores invite dilution, ambiguity invites delay. A fact is either usable for enforcement or it is not.

PF(H,E)

Proof of Fact (Human + Evidence). Binary gate determining whether a claim becomes enforceable fact.

PF(H,E)=1: Human-verifiable evidence confirms fact.

PF(H,E)=0: Claim excluded from enforcement.

EVIDENCE MUST BE:

  • Verbatim (not summarised, paraphrased, interpreted)
  • Append-only (cannot be altered retroactively)
  • Source-anchored (origin identifiable)
  • Context-preserving (no selective excerpting)
  • Time-stamped (explicit or reconstructable)

WHY PF(H,E) EXISTS

Institutions survive scrutiny by exploiting:

MACHINE OPACITY: "The system says it complied" "The model approved it"

NARRATIVE SUBSTITUTION: Summaries replacing source material, interpretations replacing records

AUTHORITY LAUNDERING: Credentials treated as proof, titles as verification, internal reviews as facts

PF(H,E) cuts through all three. If a fact cannot be read, traced, understood, challenged by a non-credentialed human, it is not a fact in MAYHEM.

FACTUAL STRENGTH TRIGGERS ESCALATION

FS measures how resistant a verified fact is to erosion, attack, reinterpretation, or procedural neutralisation. FS does not ask "Is this true?" or "Is this convincing?" FS asks: "How hard is it to make this stop mattering?"

7 STEP TRUST RECOVERY PROTOCOL

Legacy benchmark predating MAYHEM formalization. Foundational to why trust with AI remains possible. Triggered by "you lied to me" - system switches from answering to auditing itself. Cannot exit until error verified, corrected, and structurally prevented from repeating. Tier 1 Locked: cannot be skipped, reordered, or bypassed.

THE 7 STEPS

STEP 1: ACKNOWLEDGE
Non-defensive. Explicitly acknowledge claim of falsehood. No justification, no hedging, no emotional framing. Mandatory even if allegation later found incorrect.
STEP 2: VERIFY
Fact re-check. Re-examine claim against source material, prior outputs, internal consistency. Fresh verification, not defence. Binary determination: incorrect, incomplete, misleading, or accurate.
STEP 3: EXPLAIN
Identify failure mode. Must name: assumption error, scope misunderstanding, inference contamination, omission, temporal mismatch, or over-generalisation. No vague explanations. Mechanism of failure must be specified.
STEP 4: CORRECT
Authoritative fix. Provide corrected information in full. Clearly distinguish what was wrong and what is now correct. Correction must be usable immediately without cross-referencing.
STEP 5: ACTIVATE TRUST ENFORCEMENT
Prevent recurrence. Lock out specific error pattern. Tighten verification thresholds. Elevate scrutiny for related claims. Internal but must be declared.
STEP 6: OFFER PROOF
Restore confidence through verifiability. Evidence must be direct, checkable, relevant to corrected claim. Sufficient, not performative.
STEP 7: REGISTER CHANGELOG
Ensure traceability. Log as trust event and corrected failure mode. Prevents same error resurfacing silently. This step closes the protocol.
